Prosper Visualization

Prosper Marketplace, Inc. is a San Francisco, California-based company in the peer-to-peer lending industry. The company operates Prosper.com, a website where individuals can either invest in personal loans or request to borrow money. It offers unsecured personal loans up to $35,000 at rates that depend on the borrower's credit rating. Each loan is typically funded by multiple people, suggesting a marketplace for loans (for all states except Maine, Iowa, and North Dakota.). They charge borrowers a fee to take out a loan, calling it a closing fee (for most loans the fee is 5% of the loan amount).

The story that this visualization focuses on is the remarkable evolution of Proper Marketplace's portfolio of loans over its first 8 year: Beginning with the type of loans that it offered, their value, and their geographic location, the visualization then focuses on the the pattern of yields on those loans, in aggregate, (as well as the status of those loans - whether they defaulted or were completed successfully) as the company moved from being a startup to a company that orchestrates hundreds of millions of dollars in loans.

Prosper Loan Data

The data used in this visualization comprises 113,937 observations, which represent individual loans, spanning 2005-11-09 to 2014-03-10. For each loan there are 81 variables, including information on: Loan Origination Date; Loan Origination Amount; Multiple variables describing the borrower (including credit rating, occupation, income, employment status); Multiple variables describing the rate of return/payments on the loan (i.e. from both the borrowers and investors perspective); and Multiple variables describing the current state of the loan (including deliquency, default, charge-off, closed).

The first loan origination was on the 15th of September, 2005. The total dollar value of loans issued, over the time period of the dataset, was $949,894,347, or just under $1 billion. The average loan issued was $8,339

Company Information Relevant to Data Analysis:
“On November 24, 2008, the SEC found Prosper to be in violation of the Securities Act of 1933. As a result of these findings, the SEC imposed a cease and desist order on Prosper … In July 2009, Prosper reopened their website for lending ("investing") and borrowing after having obtained SEC registration for its loans ("notes"). After the relaunch, bidding on loans was restricted to residents of 28 U.S. states and the District of Columbia . Borrowers may reside in any of 47 states, with residents of three states (Iowa, Maine, and North Dakota) not permitted to borrow through Prosper”. Source: Wikipedia

So that any analysis performed over time will show this gap in trading.

Loans By State, For Each Year & Loan Category

Both the main and alternative visualizations are interactive displays for visualizing loan categories issued by Prosper for each state for each year. The categories (or purpose) for loans made were: Auto & Home, Business, Debt, Medical & Personal, Other/NA, Student & Taxes.

Notable Features:

  1. The rapid increase in loans facilitated, from $64 million in 2007 to over $360 million in 2013 (despite not offering services for a large part of 2009). The 2014 data is for a partial year, up to March 10th.
  2. The main category of loans facilitated by Prosper was for debt refinancing.
  3. The growth is broadly proportional across states.

Estimated Actual Yield By Credit Rating, Monthly

As we have seen, Prosper facilitated a total of $949,894,300 in loans, mainly to refinance debt. Each loan created has a stated Lender's Yield. In this section, the actual Lender's Yield is estimated.

Estimating the actual Lender's Yield implies using only loans that are not current - which totaled $363,719,700. As can be seen in the companion Loan Status visualization, this means that the estimated yield for more recent years is based on a smaller and smaller proportion of loans (and so cannot be considered to be a true reflection of the yield for the relevant portfolio of loans displayed for those years). This is the reason for the shaded region in the main visualization, which represents years for which complete loans are less than 50% of total loans. The Loan Status visualization also highlights that the relative proportion of completed to defaulted loans closely tracks the estimated yield.

Customer payments, for loans that were not current, were $335,204,900, which suggests a gross (non-annualized) return of -7.84% (or -3.97% when recovery is included). Pre-2009, including recovery, there was a gross return of -5.73% (on $178,560,500 of loans).

Calculation of accumulated return (r) and annualized return (ra).

For each subset of data in the visualization, the annualized return (ra), is calculated as:

$$\begin{array}{rcl} r &=& {{\textrm{Customer Payments - Service Fees - Loan Amount}} \over \textrm{Loan Amount}}\\ & \textrm{or, } & \textrm{ for defaulted/charged off loans}\\ &=& {\textrm{Customer Payments - Service Fees - Collection Fees - Loan Amount + Non-principal Recovery + Principal Recovery} \over \textrm{Loan Amount}}\\ r_{a} &=& \left( 1 + r \right)^{\frac{12}{\textrm{Actual Term}}}\\ \end{array} $$

Notable Features:

  1. The pattern of yields for lower risk loans show no notable pattern over time.
  2. The pattern of yields for higher risk loans show a marked change when comparing the pre-2009 and post-2009 periods. This suggests a change in buiness practices after having obtained SEC registration for its loans.
